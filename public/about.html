Internet: that new thing!

Mid '90s

Domestic Internet was getting into people's life and email was a new big thing: fast, cost-free...

Back then only pioneering companies like Amazon and eBay did online business, the rest just had nice catalogs at most!

More

Why not selling there?

End '90s

It was a matter of time! Shiny web catalogs, showcasing colorful products gathered more and more visitors to brand's websites!

Where better to try and sell than right where your customers look for you?

It was not a crazy idea anymore to give your credit card details and receive your products some days after that at a discounted price, since a website saved the need for a physical location!

More
Beginning '2000s

The .com bubble!

Internet was an new amazing distribution channel and SMBs began join it by selling online!

Just like that? Well, no, that is the up side!

The downside was that, to get built, online stores required back then all the things (plus paperwork) mentioned at the beginning of this story and the tools were more complex to use.

Online stores had to be built ad hoc from scratch! I built a couple of them on my own! (Feeling a bit of a grandpa now... lol)
